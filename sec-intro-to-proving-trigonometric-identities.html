    Subsection 10.1.1 General Principles for Proving Identities

    • Convert all trigonometric expressions to sine and cosine. All trigonometric ratios can be written in terms of the ``basic" trig ratios sine and cosine.
      The one exception is if the entire identity only includes tangent and/or cotangent, in which case it is usually simpler to convert everything in terms of tangent.
